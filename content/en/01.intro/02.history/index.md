## Not a whole lot has changed, or has it?

The truth is, the framework is still the same in essence. The core was rewritten a few years ago to leverage TypeScript and the core team has grown and changed members a little bit.

Today, Vue 2 is deprecated. I used it professionally for a couple years and was in the middle of the "Vue 3 is around the corner, it's just that it will take a little while still" era. Those were some rough times and it was a little difficult to find for example, good component libraries that were ready to use. Where Vuetify reigned in Vue 2, it was somewhat hard to come by something that was good for prod.

Fortunately, like I said: the essence was kept, the syntax evolved as we'll see in later chapters and lessons, but even the "old way" (called Options API) to write Vue code is supported to this day. Great news!

We didn't end up at a Python2 vs Python3 situation.
