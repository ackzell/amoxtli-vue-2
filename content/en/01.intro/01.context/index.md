# Context

As mentioned, this is a new attempt at creating material to learn Vue. And let me explain a little about the ideas that revolve that goal of mine to share a bit of knowledge about the framework with you.

Ever since I started creating material to learn Vue, a few things have changed: new frameworks came to life, but not at the same frenetic pace as they once were. Also, not all of them have approached building UIs for the web fundamentally differently (except maybe for [qwik](https://qwik.dev/), yet it shares syntax with React, but you can read about that on your own 😝), the rise of the :tooltip-trigger{id="meta-frameworks"}[meta-frameworks] that are built on top of frontend frameworks such as Vue, to enable developing features for web apps with more ease by providing for instance routing out of the box, more tooling and sometimes built-in opinions on architecture has been almost a natural evolution of the technology and they are now part of our every day lives.

There is also a latent, almost crushing reality that the AI is taking over every single aspect of our lives, and that very much includes the _programming_ industry. I still feel like we should learn to use the underlying tools in order to achieve better results with the higher level ones. At this point in time it's almost certain that you've heard about MCPs and agents and prompts. I won't bore you with my own thoughts about that in this part but just wanted to say: I want you to succeed in using those tools and to be able to direct the efforts of your programming agent when you're building your own apps. For that, IMHO you will need a good grasp on what tech you are having it use. Vue is the perfect example of one piece of tech that can be leveraged for spinning up apps very quickly because of its easy learning curve, excellent performance and the fact that is usually :tooltip-trigger{id="surveys"}[ranked pretty high] in developer experience satisfaction when developers are asked about it.

But you are likely already sold on learning the framework and reading me rant about stuff is not the reason why you are here. So let's get to it and start learning some Vue.js, shall we?
